History

Phi Gamma Nu was founded on February 17th, 1924 on Northwestern University’s Chicago campus. Originally only open to females, Sylvia Pekar Arnold and five other women joined together to form the first chapter.

In 1974, in accordance to Title IX, Phi Gamma Nu opened its doors to men to become one of the nation’s premier co-educational fraternities. Phi Gamma Nu is known as a force for professionalism, service, and brotherhood.

Established in 2013, Phi Gamma Nu Delta Upsilon strives to make a difference in the St. Louis Community. Through Philanthropy, Phi Gamma Nu chapters are able to build strong bonds with their respective communities as well. As the fraternity's brothers believe in civic responsibility, chapters nationwide organize and participate in community outreach events like fundraisers and working with local animal shelters. We also work hard to make and maintain relationships with the other business fraternities and organizations to cultivate friendships and a strong professional network. Feel free to further explore our activities through our three pillars.

Purpose

Phi Gamma Nu was founded for the purpose of fostering the study of business in colleges and universities; to uphold the interest of our Alma Maters through the encouragement of high scholarship, participation in school activities, and the association of students for their mutual advancement; to promote professional competency and achievement in the field of business; and to further a high standard of commercial ethics and culture in civic and professional enterprises.

Vision

  • To provide an environment outside the classroom where students might broaden their understanding of business.
  • To foster the professional development of students through tours, forums, speakers and research projects.
  • To stress the importance of high professional ethics, rigid standards and exemplary practices.
  • To encourage chapters to set up committees that promote involvement in social, community, professional and fund raising activities.
  • To encourage chapters to follow the rules established by the National Bylaws.
  • To prepare student members for the transition from the college atmosphere to the business world.
  • To unite faculty, students, and business professionals through common affiliation in a fraternal organization.
  • To be a medium through which students with common professional interests can develop lasting relationships among themselves and with practicing professionals.
